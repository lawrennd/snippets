\newslides{Agentic debt}

\slidesincremental{
* Agentic AI can *pay down* technical and intellectual debt: compressing bandwidth, making work legible, and reducing coordination overhead.
* But it can create *agentic debt*: delegation without clear authority boundaries, least-privilege tooling, and reversible actions.
* Pay it down with explicit instruction hierarchy, audited tool calls, and rollback-by-default workflows.
}

\notes{This is the “new debt” introduced by systems that can act: the accrued risk and cost of operating delegated workflows without crisp boundaries. Unlike technical debt (maintenance shortcuts) and intellectual debt (explanation/understanding), agentic debt is about unsafe or illegible delegation—who (or what) can cause what action, on what evidence, with what recovery path.}
